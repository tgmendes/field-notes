Context

The market has changed
dramatically.

Two years ago, Roomba was the default answer. Today, Xiaomi-backed brands Roborock and Dreame dominate Reddit's r/RobotVacuums, and the consensus is that iRobot is increasingly irrelevant while Shark is universally panned. If you came from a Roomba j7 and hated the navigation — you're not alone, and the good news is you won't repeat that experience with any of the picks below.

LiDAR navigation is now table stakes on anything worth buying. The real differentiator in 2025–2026 is obstacle avoidance — RGB cameras that recognize cables, socks, pet toys, and shoes. This is where brands differ most, and where the Roomba j7 famously fell short.

On self-emptying: the best docks now auto-wash mop pads with hot water, hot-air dry them, refill water tanks, and bag dust for up to 10 weeks. Maintenance genuinely becomes nearly zero.

🗺️

Navigation

LiDAR + RGB camera is the winning combination. LiDAR alone maps well; the camera handles real-world obstacles.

01
🥇 Best All-Rounder

Roborock Qrevo CurvX

~$850–1,000 on sale · ~$1,100 retail

The Reddit consensus pick for anyone coming from a bad navigation experience. It has 22,000 Pa suction, a zero-tangle dual brush system, a retractable LiDAR that dips under low furniture, and an AdaptiLift chassis that climbs thresholds up to 4 cm. The dock hot-washes mop pads at 80°C, air-dries them, auto-empties dust for up to 10 weeks, and self-cleans.

The app is widely regarded as the best in the business — multi-floor mapping, no-go zones, room-level scheduling, and Matter compatibility. Customer support from Roborock is also frequently cited as a reason to choose them over Dreame.

Strengths

  • Best-in-class app experience
  • Excellent obstacle avoidance
  • Zero-tangle dual brush
  • Very thorough dock automation
  • Strong customer support

Tradeoffs

  • More expensive than Dreame equivalents
  • Conservative corner cleaning algorithm
  • Struggles with some cables on carpet

Best if: You want a proven, reliable all-rounder with an excellent app, the best customer support, and you don't mind paying a modest premium.

02
🥈 Best Value

Dreame L40 Ultra

~$400–600 on sale · ~$800 retail

Arguably the best dollar-per-feature robot vacuum available right now, and it's been dropping dramatically in price. It has an RGB camera for genuinely impressive obstacle avoidance (often rated above the Qrevo Curv series at this task), and critically, it can detach its mop pads entirely while vacuuming carpet — a real advantage the Roborock doesn't offer at this level.

Reddit's r/RobotVacuums calls it a "phenomenal robot that will do anything you could possibly think of asking." Its dock hot-washes pads, auto-empties, and dries. The extending side brush reaches corners aggressively.

Strengths

  • Superior obstacle avoidance (RGB camera)
  • Detachable mops for carpet passes
  • Excellent value — often 50%+ off
  • Great edge & corner cleaning
  • Full dock automation included

Tradeoffs

  • Buggy app vs. Roborock
  • Dark rugs can confuse sensors
  • Dock mop cleaning less thorough
  • Weaker warranty support

Best if: You have mostly hard floors, value obstacle avoidance over all else, and want to save a few hundred dollars. When it's on sale below $500, it's almost impossible to say no.

Roborock Saros 10R

~$1,000–1,100 on sale

The true flagship if navigation is your absolute top priority. At just 3.14 inches tall with a retractable LiDAR, it gets under furniture most robots can't reach. It earned a perfect 24/24 score in independent obstacle avoidance testing — the highest ever recorded — making it the Mid-2025 Vacuum Wars Obstacle Avoidance Award winner. The full dock matches everything the CurvX offers.

Strengths

  • Perfect obstacle avoidance (24/24)
  • Ultra-slim profile, goes under everything

  • Full dock automation

Tradeoffs

  • Most expensive of the three
  • Overkill for simple floor plans

Best if: You have a cluttered home, cable chaos, or simply want the absolute best navigation money can buy without reaching absurd price territory.

04
💧 Best Mopping

Eufy Omni E25

~$700–900

If mopping quality matters most, the Eufy E25 and E28 with their HydroJet roller mop design are the clear winners in head-to-head mopping tests — beating both Dreame and Roborock on dried spills and liquid messes. The roller mop applies constant fresh water and extracts dirty water in one pass, unlike spinning pads that redistribute dirt.

The tradeoff: roller mops are bulkier, can struggle with hair getting inside the mechanism, and Eufy's long-term customer support is more mixed than Roborock's.

Strengths

  • Best mopping performance overall
  • Roller mop cleans grout/tile well
  • Strong vacuuming on hard floors
  • Good price-to-feature ratio

Tradeoffs

  • Bulkier roller mop mechanism
  • More mixed long-term support
  • Skips mixed wet/dry obstacles

Best if: You have tile or hardwood floors and want the cleanest mop result. If you're skeptical that mopping is a gimmick — this one might change your mind.

On Mopping

A note on mopping

It's gotten good.
Really good.

Skepticism about robot mopping is completely reasonable — a couple of years ago, it was mostly a damp cloth dragged around the floor. That era is over.

Modern high-end docks automatically wash mop pads with hot water (up to 80°C on the CurvX, 70°C on the S5X), spin-dry them with heated air, and self-clean the dock tray so residue doesn't build up. The Eufy E25's HydroJet roller continuously supplies clean water and extracts dirty water in a single pass.

The result: if you run the mop daily on hard floors, they legitimately stay clean in ways that feel surprising the first few weeks.

The caveats: spinning mop pads (Roborock, Dreame) are better at avoiding carpet and lower maintenance overall. Roller mops (Eufy, Narwal) are better at scrubbing tile grout and removing stuck-on messes, but the rollers can get gunked up with hair and need occasional manual cleaning.

Bottom line: If you have mostly hard floors, buy a model with mopping. You'll use it. If you have mostly carpet, mopping is a nice-to-have but not worth paying extra for.

What to Avoid

What to avoid

Save yourself the pain.

🚫

iRobot / Roomba

iRobot went through bankruptcy in 2026 and was acquired by Shenzhen-based Picea Robotics. The brand is in flux, customer support is unreliable, and newer models don't compete on navigation. The j7 was already the Reddit community's poster child for bad navigation.

🚫

Shark

Universally panned on r/RobotVacuums. Poor navigation, poor battery life, cheap construction, and inadequate customer support. Multiple Reddit users who tried them returned within a week.

⚠️

Ecovacs Deebot (use caution)

Some Deebot models offer compelling specs at good prices, but Reddit users frequently cite worse navigation consistency than Roborock and Dreame. The X8 Pro Omni tested well in 2025, but it's an exception.

⚠️

Dreame X50 Ultra (flagship)

Multiple Reddit users reported the X50 Ultra dying within days, battery drain issues, and navigation failures near dark rugs and in low light. The older, cheaper L40 Ultra actually performs better for everyday use.
