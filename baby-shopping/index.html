Category 01 Sleep

Where baby sleeps safely is the single most important purchase you'll make. Focus on a firm, flat surface and safe sleep guidelines (back to sleep, nothing in the crib).

Bassinet / Moses basket Must
For the first 3–6 months, a bedside bassinet is far more practical than waking up for cot feeds. Look for one with a firm, flat mattress and good ventilation.
Pick: Halo Bassinest Swivel Alt: SnüzPod 4
Cot / crib (0–3 yrs) Must
Buy one that converts to a toddler bed to get more mileage from it. Ensure slat spacing meets current safety standards.
Pick: IKEA Sundvik Alt: Stokke Sleepi (pricey but grows with child)
Cot mattress Must
Firm and flat — don't skimp here. Should fit snugly with no gaps around the edges. Breathable fabric is a bonus.
Pick: Naturepedic Organic Budget: IKEA Tullugon
2× fitted cot sheets Must
You'll change these at 3am more than you expect. 100% cotton, fitted only — no loose bedding in the sleep space.
Sleeping bags / swaddle sacks (2–3) Must
Safer than blankets and much easier once baby starts rolling. Get 0–6m and 6–18m. Look for 2.5 tog for year-round use.
Pick: Ergobaby Swaddle Alt: Halo SleepSack
White noise machine Nice
A game changer for sleep — masks household noise. Many parents swear by it; others find a free app works just as well for newborns.
Pick: Hatch Rest 2nd Gen Budget: LectroFan
Baby monitor Must
Video + audio for peace of mind. Some parents prefer a dedicated camera over a phone app for reliability. Breathing sensors are optional but popular.
Pick: Nanit Pro Alt: Infant Optics DXR-8 (no WiFi = no hacking risk)
Blackout blinds / curtains Must
Critical for daytime naps and early morning wake-ups. Portable travel ones are great too.
Pick: Gro Anywhere Blind (travel) Permanent: IKEA Majgull

Category 02 Feeding

Breastfeeding, formula, or combo — your list will look different depending on your plan. Buy bottles even if breastfeeding; having options matters when you're exhausted.

Breast pump Must
Even if you plan to primarily breastfeed, a pump gives you flexibility. Wearable pumps are a revelation for working mums. Check if it's covered by your health insurance.
Wearable: Elvie Stride Workhorse: Medela Pump In Style Budget: Spectra S2
Nursing/feeding pillow Must
Saves your back during feeding sessions. Also useful as a tummy-time prop later.
Pick: Boppy Original Alt: My Brest Friend
Bottles (4–6) Must
Start with smaller 150ml bottles, then move to 250ml. Anti-colic designs reduce fussiness. Try 1–2 types before buying a full set.
Anti-colic: Dr. Brown's Options+ Simple: Tommee Tippee Closer to Nature Breastfed babies: Nanobebe
Haakaa / silicone pump Nice
Catches letdown on the opposite breast during feeding. Builds a freezer stash passively. Cheap and wildly useful.
Pick: Haakaa Gen 2 (150ml)
Bottle steriliser Must
Microwave or electric steam sterilisers are fastest. Or use a dishwasher + boiling water. Don't overthink it.
Pick: Philips Avent 3-in-1 Budget: Tommee Tippee Microwave
Nursing bras (3–4) Must
Get fitted in the last trimester — your size will change. Sleep nursing bras are different from day bras; get both.
Pick: Cake Maternity Budget: Kindred Bravely
Breast pads (disposable or reusable) Must
You'll go through these constantly in the first weeks. Stock up before birth.
High chair Must
Not needed until ~6 months (weaning). Can wait a little. Look for one that's easy to clean — avoid fabric seat pads.
Pick: Stokke Tripp Trapp (grows to adult) Budget: IKEA Antilop Compact: Joie Mimzy Snacker
Muslin squares (10+) Must
The single most versatile thing you'll buy. Burp cloth, makeshift swaddle, shade cloth, changing mat cover, drool catcher. Buy more than you think you need.
Formula (one starter tin) Nice
Even if breastfeeding, having a tin on hand removes stress. Don't over-buy a brand before baby arrives — some babies are fussy about formula.
Pick: Kendamil Organic Alt: HiPP Combiotic

Category 03 Diapering

You'll do roughly 8–12 nappy changes per day in the early weeks. Set up a comfortable changing station — it's worth it.

Nappies / diapers — newborn + size 1 (small stock) Must
Don't overbuy newborn size — babies grow fast. Get 1–2 packs of newborn, then a larger stock of size 1.
Pick: Pampers Pure Budget: Mamia (Aldi) Eco: Naty or Kit & Kin
Baby wipes (unscented, water-based) Must
Buy in bulk. WaterWipes are popular for newborns' sensitive skin; cheaper alternatives work fine once skin settles.
Pick: WaterWipes Budget: Pampers Sensitive
Changing mat Must
A waterproof mat that wipes clean — not a padded one that absorbs mess. Get 2 if you want one upstairs and one downstairs.
Pick: Lindam Safety First
Nappy cream / barrier cream Must
Use at every change to prevent nappy rash. Zinc oxide-based creams are most effective.
Pick: Bepanthen Nappy Rash Ointment Alt: Sudocrem
Nappy bin / nappy sacks Nice
A dedicated nappy bin kills the smell. Not essential if you have a lidded bin nearby and empty often. Nappy sacks are a cheaper alternative.
Pick: Tommee Tippee Sangenic
Nappy changing bag Must
Something with multiple compartments, a wipe-clean interior, and ideally a built-in changing mat. Doesn't need to say "baby bag."
Pick: Béaba Geneva Budget: Mamas & Papas

Category 04 Clothing

Buy less than you think. Babies grow out of sizes in weeks, not months. Prioritise easy access for nappy changes: popper-front or envelope-neck designs.

Sleepsuits / babygrows (7–10) Must
The workwear of babies. Get mostly 0–3m; a couple of newborn just in case. Zip-up styles are much faster at 3am than poppers.
Onesies / bodysuits (6–8) Must
Goes under everything. Envelope neck (not just round neck) makes getting them on and off much easier.
Socks (6–8 pairs) Must
They fall off constantly. Buy more than you think. Anti-slip soles matter once they start standing.
Scratch mitts (2–3 pairs) Must
Newborns have sharp nails and no coordination. Mitts prevent face-scratching in the early weeks.
Hat (2–3) Must
Hospitals and midwives will want a hat on the baby immediately. Newborns lose heat through their heads quickly.
Cardigan / light jacket (2) Nice
For layering — useful when going in and out of shops or cars. Avoid bulky snowsuits with car seat buckling.
Going-home outfit Nice
Mostly for photos. Make it practical — easy poppers, warm enough for the season. Don't overthink it.

Category 05 Bathing

Newborns only need bathing 2–3 times a week. Keep it simple — a tub insert, a few essentials, and you're set.

Baby bath tub or bath support Must
A dedicated tub is safest for newborns. Look for one with a newborn insert that props them up safely.
Pick: Angelcare Baby Bath Support Alt: Stokke Flexi Bath
Baby wash & shampoo Must
Fragrance-free, pH neutral formulas. Avoid anything with SLS in the early months.
Pick: Childs Farm Baby Wash Alt: Weleda Calendula
Hooded towels (2) Must
Keeps baby's head warm immediately after the bath. Larger than you think they need — babies grow into them fast.
Soft washcloths (4–6) Must
For face washes between baths too. Cotton muslin works just as well as dedicated baby ones.
Baby nail file / clippers Must
Newborn nails are razor-sharp and grow fast. An electric nail file (like the Frida Baby) is far easier than clippers for tiny newborn nails.
Pick: Frida Baby NailFrida
Baby moisturiser Nice
Newborns often have dry, peeling skin — totally normal. An unfragranced lotion helps if skin is particularly dry.
Pick: Aveeno Baby Daily Moisture

Category 06 Travel & On the Go

The car seat is legally required and non-negotiable. The pram is the biggest investment — try before you buy and check it fits in your car boot.

Infant car seat (Group 0+) Must
Cannot leave the hospital without one. Must be rear-facing. ISOFIX is significantly safer and easier than belt-only. Check compatibility with your car.
Pick: Cybex Cloud T i-Size (rotating) Alt: Maxi-Cosi Pebble 360 Budget: Joie i-Gemm 3
Pram / pushchair Must
The biggest decision. Think about: boot space, terrain you'll cover, and whether you want it from birth or just for older babies. Travel systems (pram + car seat) reduce transfer faff.
Pick: UPPAbaby Vista V3 (grows with child) Urban: Bugaboo Fox 5 Budget: Joie Versatrax
Baby carrier / sling Must
Keeps baby calm and your hands free. Soft structured carriers (SSC) are easier for beginners; ring slings are more compact. Check ergonomic positioning (M-shape legs).
Pick: Ergobaby Omni 360 Structured: BabyBjörn One Air Wrap: Stretchy wrap for newborns
Rain cover for pram Must
If you're in a rainy climate, this is non-negotiable. Check it's compatible with your pram model.
Sunshade / parasol for pram Nice
Clip-on pram parasols provide better shade than built-in hoods. Check UPF rating.
Portable travel cot Nice
If you'll travel or visit family. Many double as a playard. Don't buy one just for the house.
Pick: Joie Kubbie Go Alt: BabyBjörn Travel Cot Light

Category 07 Health & Safety

A few essentials to handle common situations confidently at home without an emergency GP visit.

Digital thermometer Must
A rectal thermometer is most accurate for newborns but any fast-reading digital model works. Forehead strip thermometers are inaccurate — avoid.
Pick: Braun ThermoScan 7 (ear)
Infant paracetamol & ibuprofen Must
Stock before baby arrives — you don't want to be out at midnight. Check age/weight limits on the packaging.
Note: ibuprofen only from 3 months+
Nasal aspirator Must
Babies can't blow their nose. A blocked nose means they can't feed. The Frida NoseFrida (snot sucker) is the cult pick — it sounds gross but parents swear by it.
Pick: Frida Baby NoseFrida
Saline nasal drops Must
Helps clear congestion before using the aspirator. Use sterile saline only.
Baby first aid kit Must
Plasters, gauze, antiseptic wipes, tweezers, oral syringe for medicines. Pre-made kits are convenient or build your own.
Stair gates (2) Nice
Not needed until 6–9 months when baby starts moving. Top of stairs is critical. Pressure-mounted gates are fine for bottom of stairs; screw-fixed only at the top.
Pick: Dreambaby Retractable Gate
Socket covers + corner guards Nice
Again, not urgent for newborns but plan ahead. Do a room audit before they become mobile.
Colic/wind drops Nice
Infacol or Gripe Water can help with wind pain — evidence is mixed, but many parents find it helpful.
Pick: Infacol (from birth)

Category 08 Play & Development

Babies don't need much in the early weeks. Faces are their favourite toy. But a few items make a big difference for your sanity and their development.

Play mat with arch / activity gym Must
Essential for tummy time and independent play from about 6 weeks. Look for high-contrast patterns (babies can't see colour yet).
Pick: Lovevery Play Gym Budget: Skip Hop Explore & More
Baby bouncer Must
A safe spot to put baby down while you eat/shower. Babies love gentle bouncing. Look for one with recline settings.
Pick: Babybjörn Bouncer Bliss Budget: Fisher-Price Infant-to-Toddler Rocker
Swing / rocker (electric) Nice
Some babies love swings, others couldn't care less. A game-changer for colicky babies. Try before buying if possible — they take up a lot of space.
Pick: 4moms MamaRoo 4 Budget: Fisher-Price Sweet Snugapuppy
High-contrast books (board books) Nice
Black and white patterns stimulate newborn vision from day one. Simple and cheap.
Rattles / soft toys Nice
Simple, lightweight rattles and soft toys for grasping. Don't overbuy — you'll receive plenty as gifts.
Teething toys Nice
Not needed until 4–6 months when teething begins. Have some ready by then.
Pick: Sophie la Girafe (classic)
Dummy / pacifier (2–3) Nice
Evidence shows dummies can reduce SIDS risk. Orthodontic designs are better for teeth. Introduce after breastfeeding is established (3–4 weeks).
Pick: Philips Avent Soothie Alt: BIBS (popular aesthetically)

Category 09 Nursery

The nursery doesn't need to be perfect before baby arrives. But a few things make night feeds much less brutal.

Nursing / glider chair Must
You will spend hundreds of hours in this chair. Get one that's comfortable for your height, easy to clean, and ideally rocks or glides. Don't skip this.
Pick: Dutailier Glider Budget: IKEA Poäng (add cushion)
Chest of drawers / wardrobe Must
The IKEA Hemnes or Sundvik range works well and doubles as a changing table with a mat on top — saving you buying a separate unit.
Night light (amber/red) Must
Lets you navigate for night feeds without waking yourself up fully. Amber or red light doesn't disrupt melatonin like white/blue light.
Pick: Hatch Rest (adjustable colour) Budget: IKEA Spoka or a simple plug-in amber light
Room thermometer Must
Ideal nursery temperature is 16–20°C. A simple digital one costs next to nothing and removes the guesswork on what to dress baby in for sleep.
Humidifier Nice
Particularly useful in winter when heating dries out the air. Helps with congestion too. Use distilled water to prevent mould.
Pick: Frida Baby 3-in-1 Humidifier
Storage baskets / organiser Nice
For nappies, wipes, and small items at the changing station. Having everything in reach saves you mid-change disasters.
Laundry hamper Nice
Baby clothes are small but plentiful. A dedicated hamper for the nursery keeps things contained.
